The Community-Based Multimodal Early Warning System (CB-MCEWS) is an innovative solution developed to address the critical lack of timely and effective disaster warnings in vulnerable and underserved communities. Many indigenous and remote populations face severe risks from natural hazards such as floods, earthquakes, hurricanes, landslides, and heatwaves due to limited access to infrastructure, technology, and reliable communication systems. The absence of early warnings often results in significant loss of life, displacement of populations, destruction of livelihoods, and long-term economic damage.

This project aims to design a scalable, inclusive, and culturally aware early warning system that prioritizes human safety, community participation, and resilience building. The system is tailored to meet the unique needs of vulnerable communities by ensuring accessibility, trust, and usability, even in low-resource environments
